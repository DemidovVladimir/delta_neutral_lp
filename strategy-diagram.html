What this means for "how often": the bot makes 2 on-chain RPC reads + 1 Jupiter price-API read per tick (twice a minute). It writes one snapshot row to the PnL DB per tick. Rebalances and swaps happen only when the imbalance branch fires — typically tens of minutes to hours apart, depending on volatility.

Validation Checklist for "Are We Swapping/Rebalancing Too Often?"

  • Run pnpm pnl — the "Recent rebalances" list shows trigger composition (sol % / usdc %) per event. Two rebalances with composition near 80 % suggest the threshold is too aggressive for current vol; raise AUTO_TUNE_IMBALANCE_THRESHOLD.
  • "Recent swaps" with context = retry-rebalance rows mean Phase 2 retries are repeatedly topping up — usually because slippage ate into the first swap. Bump SWAP_SLIPPAGE_BUFFER_PCT.
  • Failed swaps (✗ rows) by direction tell you whether the wallet under-funds one side; check the per-position scaling banner in the orchestrator log.
  • Compare lifetime realized PnL against HODL-only-SOL and HODL-50/50 PnL — if the LP is losing to HODL-50/50 over several positions, you're paying impermanent loss faster than fees compound.
  • Compare network fees paid against LP fees claimed — the bot must out-earn its own friction. If fees-paid > fees-claimed for several cycles, the rebalance cadence is too tight.
