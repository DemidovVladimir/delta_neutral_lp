Model assumptions & limitations (read me)

The simulation is intentionally simplified — its job is to surface relative behaviour between two configurations, not to predict absolute returns. Key assumptions:

  • Price model: geometric Brownian motion with constant daily vol. No regime changes, no jumps. Real SOL has fat tails and clustered volatility.
  • Fee accrual: linear in time-in-range with a 20/binCount capital-efficiency multiplier on the supplied APR. Real DLMM fees concentrate at the active bin and depend on actual trading volume distribution.
  • IL realisation: at each rebalance the position composition is approximated linearly within range (100% Y above range, 100% X below range, linear in-between). Real DLMM bin-level math is closer to a square-root curve but the broad shape matches.
  • Rebalance cost: $0.20 fixed gas + 2-30 bps of swap notional. Adjust by changing your bot's SEND_OPTIMIZED setting and slippage buffer.
  • Out-of-range: position is 100% one token, earns zero fees. Triggers a rebalance only when imbalance threshold is crossed (default 92%).
  • Slippage: assumed 5 bps + (notional / 100k USD) bps to model thinning depth at larger swap sizes.

Use the simulator to sanity-check your intuition about which bin count wins each regime, then verify on real data with pnpm pnl after running each configuration on mainnet for ≥ 24 h.
